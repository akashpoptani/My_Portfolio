    Teaching Assistant for CS103: Introduction to Programming Course

    As a Teaching Assistant (TA) for the CS103: Introduction to Programming Course, I played a pivotal role in facilitating the academic journey of first-year undergraduate students. My responsibilities encompassed a wide range of tasks aimed at ensuring a comprehensive understanding of fundamental programming concepts. Here are some key aspects of my role:

    1. Guidance and Lab Assistance: Provided hands-on guidance and lab assistance during coding sessions, ensuring students grasped foundational programming principles, troubleshooted coding issues, clarified doubts, and progressed smoothly in their assignments.
    2. Evaluation and Feedback:Conducted assessments for over 20 students, including grading assignments and delivering constructive feedback to track their progress and enhance learning outcomes.
    3. Facilitating Collaborative Learning: Organized and moderated group discussions and study sessions to promote peer learning and foster a collaborative learning environment.
    4. Code Review: Conducted code reviews to help students improve their programming skills, emphasizing best practices and efficient coding techniques.
